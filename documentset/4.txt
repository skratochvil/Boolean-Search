<TITLE> KEY DATES LEADING TO ARAFAT'S GAZA TRIP</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> KEY DATES LEADING TO ARAFAT'S GAZA TRIP</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MIDEAST-CHRONOLOGY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>GAZA (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  PLO leader Yasser Arafat's historic return to Gaza Friday culminates years of violent struggle followed by tough negotiations that resulted in the PLO's historic peace deal with Israel last year. </P>
<P></P>
<P> Sept 13, 1993 - Israel and the PLO sign ``Declaration of Principles,'' a peace agreement outlining a plan for Palestinian self-rule in the occupied territories; PLO chairman Yasser Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin shake hands outside the White House in Washington. </P>
<P> Oct 6 - Arafat and Rabin meet in Cairo to discuss self-rule deal. </P>
<P> Dec 12 - Arafat and Rabin fail in last-ditch effort to reach agreement on security issues after lower-level negotiations. </P>
<P> Dec 13 - Israel misses deadline for start of troop withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho. </P>
<P> Dec 22 - Israel and PLO hold talks in Versailles, France. </P>
<P> Dec 29 - Israeli Foreign Minister Shimon Peres and PLO negotiator Mahmoud Abbas wind up talks in Cairo; Israel says agreement reached on security obstacles, prompting PLO denial and weeks of bickering. </P>
<P> Jan 10, 1994 - Israel-PLO talks resume in Taba, Egypt. </P>
<P> Feb 9 - Arafat and Peres sign partial agreement on implementing Declaration of Principles, focusing on procedures at border crossings of autonomous areas. </P>
<P> Feb 25 - Jewish settler kills about 30 Palestinians praying at a Hebron mosque. Gaza-Jericho talks stalled for five weeks as negotiators argue over Palestinian security in Hebron. </P>
<P> March 31 - Israel agrees to international observer force in Hebron; Gaza-Jericho talks resume. </P>
<P> April 13 - Deadline for completion of Israeli troop withdrawal passes. Troops remain in Gaza and Jericho, no sign of observer force in Hebron. </P>
<P> April 28 - Arafat and Peres, meeting in Cairo, set May 4 as target date for signing final agreement after Israel concedes some of PLO's requests for ``symbols of statehood.'' </P>
<P> April 29 - Economic agreement signed in Paris, laying framework for economic ties between future Palestinian self-governing areas and Israel. </P>
<P> May 4 - PLO and Israel sign landmark accord giving Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza Strip their first taste of self-rule since the 1967 Middle East War. Deal calls for Israeli pullout but permits army to stay in Jewish settlements covering about 40 percent of the narrow Strip. </P>
<P> May 11 - Israel begins transfer of power to the PLO by handing over a military base in the Gaza Strip to Palestinian police officers. </P>
<P> May 13 - Israel hands over Jericho to Palestinian police. </P>
<P> May 24 - Arafat cancels laws enforced by Israel in Jericho and the Gaza Strip; Israel says order violates peace accords. </P>
<P> May 30 - Israel begins releasing several hundred Palestinian prisoners as part of the peace deal with the PLO. </P>
<P> June 6 - Israeli Foreign Minister Shimon Peres, after government denials, makes public a letter agreeing not to harm Palestinian institutions in Jerusalem. </P>
<P> June 10 - Palestinians win $42 million in immediate aid from donors at a meeting in Paris, overcoming major hurdle for cash-strapped PLO to pay police and government workers in Gaza Strip and Jericho and clearing way for Arafat's visit. </P>
<P> June 30 - PLO Planning Minister and close Arafat aide Nabil Shaath says the PLO leader will cross into Gaza from Egypt at the Rafat border crossing at 2:30 p.m. Friday. </P>
<P> July 1 - Arafat crosses into Gaza at 3:13 p.m. (8:13 a.m. EDT) to a tumultuous welcome for his historic homecoming. </P>
