<TITLE> TROPICAL DEPRESSION UPGRADED TO TROPICAL STORM</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> TROPICAL DEPRESSION UPGRADED TO TROPICAL STORM</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-STORM </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>(Eds: New throughout)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>CORAL GABLES, Fla (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The season's first tropical depression was upgraded to tropical storm status Friday afternoon, as its maximum sustained winds reached 40 mph, the National Weather Service said. </P>
<P> Tropical Storm Alberto was moving northwest into the open waters of the central Gulf of Mexico at a rate near seven miles per hour, with tropical storm force winds extending outward up to 85 miles from the center. </P>
<P> Forecasters said some additional strengthening was possible over the next 24 hours. </P>
<P> They said the storm posed no threat to any land mass Friday evening. The Mexican government had dropped a tropical storm warning earlier Friday after the depression shifted its direction to a slightly more northerly path. </P>
<P> At 4:30 p.m. EDT, the storm was located in the Gulf of Mexico about 425 miles south-southeast of the mouth of the Mississippi River. </P>
<P> Tropical depressions are upgraded to Tropical Storms when they reach 39 miles per hour. </P>
<P> The depression formed off the western coast of Cuba on Thursday, dumping up to 10 inches of rain on the Caribbean nation. </P>
