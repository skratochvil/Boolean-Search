<TITLE> JAPANESE TOURIST FREED AFTER BEGGING ON KNEES</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> JAPANESE TOURIST FREED AFTER BEGGING ON KNEES</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PHILIPPINES-TOURIST </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MANILA (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A Japanese tourist arrested for slapping a female supervisor at Manila's international airport was freed after he knelt at her feet and begged forgiveness, police said Sunday. </P>
<P> Freelance film critic Akira Koike of Tokyo was charged with slander-by-deed before a prosecutor late Saturday after slapping Northwest Airlines ground staff supervisor Rina Tiam Beng during an argument, police added. </P>
<P> Koike, who was about to board a Northwest flight for Japan, had refused to pay $49 which Tiam Beng was charging him for excess baggage. </P>
<P> ``I beg forgiveness,'' Koike pleaded to Tiam Beng on his knees before officials and reporters at the prosecutor's office. The prosecutor ordered him released after his apologies were accepted. </P>
